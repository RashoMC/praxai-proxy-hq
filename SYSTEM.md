# Lead Pipeline Workflow

## Status Usage

### `LEAD`
Use `LEAD` when a company has been identified but contact readiness is still being qualified.

Move a lead into `LEAD` when:
- You only have basic firmographic data.
- Contact details are incomplete or unverified.
- The account still needs research, enrichment, or prioritization.

### `CONNECT`
Use `CONNECT` when the target is qualified and the next task is opening a channel.

Move a lead into `CONNECT` when:
- A decision-maker or strong contact is identified.
- Email, LinkedIn, phone, or referral path is ready.
- The team is preparing first-touch outreach but has not sent the core message yet.

### `MESSAGE`
Use `MESSAGE` when outreach is active and message quality, follow-up timing, and reply handling are the main work.

Move a lead into `MESSAGE` when:
- The first outbound message has been drafted or sent.
- Follow-up cadence is now the primary action.
- You are iterating on copy, objections, or personalization.

### `CLOSE`
Use `CLOSE` when the opportunity is in a decision or committed outcome state.

Move a lead into `CLOSE` when:
- A positive decision, contract, or concrete closing step is in motion.
- The lead is effectively won and should count toward conversion.
- The remaining work is deal execution rather than prospecting.

## Example Workflow

1. Add a company to `LEAD` after initial discovery and basic fit validation.
2. Move it to `CONNECT` once the right contact and channel are confirmed.
3. Move it to `MESSAGE` after the first outreach is prepared or sent.
4. Move it to `CLOSE` when the conversation becomes a real deal path with a clear closing action.

## Best Practices

- Do not move a lead forward just because time passed; move it when the dominant next action changed.
- Keep notes current at each transition so downstream agents know why the status changed.
- Log outreach and objections during `MESSAGE` so follow-ups are based on actual context.
- Return a lead to an earlier stage if required data or contact quality falls apart.
- Reserve `CLOSE` for real conversion work, not for leads that are merely warm or responsive.
